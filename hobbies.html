My Hobbies & Passions

These are the activities that shape who I am—what inspires me, challenges me, and brings me joy.

FFA & Forestry

FFA gave me some of my most rewarding high school experiences. I competed at a high level in forestry—earning first place in the state—and served as a regional officer, where I had the chance to help guide younger members and represent my peers. FFA wasn't just about contests; it was about building leadership skills, learning responsibility, and working toward goals with a team.

Reading

Reading is one of my favorite hobbies because it balances out the busyness of everyday life. Books give me both relaxation and inspiration, whether I'm getting lost in a story, learning something new, or finding a character that feels a little bit like me. I love recommending great books and talking about favorite authors with friends.

Spending Time with Friends

Back home, we'd go to trivia at Zorbaz most Thursdays—it was always fun, even if we never actually won. We'd also wander Walmart since that was one of the only things to do in town. In Duluth, I've gotten to explore so many new places with friends, both outdoors and in the city. Every adventure has made this chapter even more special.

Personal Stories

National FFA Convention

Traveling to national convention was a defining moment. I got to see the larger FFA community, connect with students from all over the country, and feel part of something bigger. Not to mention we getting to be on stage as the 2nd place team and 5th place individual in the nation in the forestry contest...

Prescribed Burn

One of the most memorable forestry experiences was participating in a prescribed burn. It was such a moment of time, where because I participate in FFA I was able to do such a cool and educational activity with friends.

The "Iditaread" Challenge

In third grade, I participated in a reading challenge called "Iditaread." It was a play on the Iditarod dog sled race that takes place in Alaska. Instead of hitting milestones in an actual race, we hit milestones in the amount of books that we read. I remember having a blast completing books and earning prizes.

The Fargo Bookstore

Visiting a charming independent bookstore in Fargo has also been a fun moment in reading for me. The store has stacks upon stacks of books on shelves and on the floor. To be able to scower the place for a book that I would read at a fairly decent price is always fun.

FFA taught me leadership, discipline, and the value of community. Reading gives me creativity, empathy, and perspective. Time with friends fills my life with laughter and belonging. Together, these hobbies represent balance: achieving goals while remembering to slow down and enjoy life.
